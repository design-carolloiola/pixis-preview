Please note this is an initial estimate. We can refine it after a discovery call, and it may evolve slightly as the project progresses. It’s meant as a starting point.

06 — Technical Q&A

You asked specific questions in Appendix B. Here are specific answers.

Which CMS do you recommend and why? What are the tradeoffs vs. alternatives?
+
Webflow. The honest reason: it's the only platform where full design control, a non-technical CMS, and a native HubSpot integration coexist without plugins, workarounds, or ongoing maintenance overhead. WordPress can check the boxes technically, but "technically possible" and "strategically right" are different things. The plugin stack required to match Webflow's native functionality creates fragility and unnecessary security surface area. Framer is excellent for portfolios; it's not the right tool when you also need lead capture, editorial publishing velocity, and GEO optimization at this scale.
How will you hit 90+ PageSpeed with this much video?
+
Video weight is a loading strategy problem, not a PageSpeed ceiling. Video is delivered through Vimeo, embedded with lazy loading so it does not affect initial page load. Every video loads a static poster frame first. The video itself only loads when the user scrolls it into view or interacts with it. Videos are never auto-loaded on mobile. All images export as WebP with lazy loading. Webflow CDN handles global asset delivery. Custom fonts are subset to only the characters used. JavaScript is deferred. I run PageSpeed Insights before I call anything done. If it is under 90, I find what is causing it and fix it.
HubSpot integration: native, API, or third-party connector?
+
Native Webflow and HubSpot app. No Zapier. No extra cost. Webflow has an official native integration with HubSpot, available directly in Workspace settings. Forms are built natively in Webflow, which means full design control, no HubSpot styling bleeding into your UI, and each form field maps directly to its corresponding HubSpot property. Before launch, I test. That is the acceptance criterion, not "it looks connected."
What does CMS editing actually look like for a non-technical team?
+
It looks like filling out a structured form, not touching a website. When your team adds a new show, they open a CMS item and fill in fields: show name, client, event type, location, images, video, quote, and related content. The page template is already built, so they are editing content, not layout. That is the point.
How do you handle SEO and AI visibility without bloating the build?
+
I approach this by structuring the content properly from the start and making sure the basics are in place. Clear hierarchy, metadata, and the key technical setup needed for search engines. The goal is to keep the build clean and efficient, without relying on unnecessary plugins or adding complexity. If needed, we can also bring in a dedicated SEO specialist to go deeper into strategy or advanced optimizations.
